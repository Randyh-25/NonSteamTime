# Steam Non-Game Playtime Manager

A simple graphical tool to manage and edit the playtime for your Non-Steam games added to your Steam Library.

---

## How to Safely Edit Playtime (Without Steam Resetting It)

> **IMPORTANT NOTE:** Steam has an aggressive Cloud Sync feature. If you simply edit your playtime while connected to the internet, Steam will think your local file is "corrupted" and automatically overwrite it with the old data from their servers. 

To make your custom playtime permanent, please follow these exact steps carefully:

### Step 1: Kill the Steam Process
Before making any changes, Steam must be completely closed.
1. Open the **Steam Non-Game Playtime Manager** app.
2. Click the red **"Kill Steam"** button at the top menu.
3. Wait for the confirmation message saying the process is terminated.

### Step 2: Edit and Save Your Playtime
1. Find the game you want to modify in the list.
2. Click the **"Edit Playtime"** button.
3. Enter your desired hours (e.g., `10.5`) and click **"Save Changes"**.
4. The app will automatically save this new data to your local Steam config file.

### Step 3: Disconnect from the Internet (Crucial Step)
To prevent Steam from immediately talking to its servers and undoing your work, you must force it into Offline Mode.
* Temporarily turn off your Wi-Fi or unplug your Ethernet cable from your PC.

### Step 4: Launch Steam in Offline Mode
1. In the app, click the green **"Launch Steam"** button (or open Steam normally from your desktop).
2. Because you have no internet connection, Steam will show a "Connection Error" or "Network Error" window.
3. Click on the **"Start in Offline Mode"** button.

### Step 5: Verify Your New Playtime
1. Once Steam opens, navigate to your **Library**.
2. Click on the Non-Steam game you just edited.
3. Check the playtime counter. It should now successfully display your new custom hours!

### Step 6: Go Back Online
Now that Steam has loaded your custom hours into its active memory, you can safely sync it back to the cloud.
1. Reconnect your internet (Turn your Wi-Fi back on or plug in the cable).
2. Inside the Steam app, click on the word **"Steam"** in the very top-left corner of the window.
3. Select **"Go Online..."** from the drop-down menu.
4. Click **"Leave Offline Mode"** to restart Steam.

---

**Congratulations!** Your new playtime is now permanently saved and synced with Steam. You only need to do this exact process whenever you want to edit the hours manually.
